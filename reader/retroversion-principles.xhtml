Digenis reader: principles for retroversion

Orthography is normalized in conformity with common (not universal) practice ca. 1200. See Horace G. Lunt, The language of Rus′ in the eleventh century: some observations about facts and theories, Harvard Ukrainian studies, 12/13 (1988), pp. 276–313 (Appendix at pp. 302–04).

Phonology and orthography

  • All jers (strong and weak) are written according to etymology, and etymological jers missing from the manuscript are restored
  • Do not write a jer after prefixes без-, из-, раз-, въз-, об-, and spell voicing assimilations where they occur (Lunt OCS grammar §3.311 ff.)
  • Jat′ and е are written according to etymology, except that third jat′ is not used (see below, under Morphology).
  • Consonant cluster adjustments in the manuscript resulting from the loss of jers in weak position, such as voicing assimilation (e.g., збысться) or degemination (e.g., многоценыя), are written according to etymology
  • The first and second velar palatalizations are spelled where expected, regardless of whether they are reflected at those locations in the manuscript.
  • Do not write й; write и instead.
  • Undo all apocope, e.g. infinitive, short reflexive pronoun, and masc sg nom/acc long adjectival endings.
  • Write щ from *tj and ж from *dj (Lunt A3)
  • Plain and soft(ening) vowel letters:
    • Write ѧ and ꙗ (or а after palatals, see below) etymologically, including ѧ instead of third jat′ (see below, under Morphology); do not use modern letterform я
    • Write only а, е, ꙋ, и (not ꙗ, ѥ, ю, ы) after ш, ж, ч, щ, ц
    • Write only ѥ (not е) initially, after vowel letters, and after historically palatal (jotated) л, н, р (e.g. глаголѥть) (but not ш, ж, ч, щ, ц; see above)
    • Write ѥ, and not о, initially in words like ѥзеро, ѥдинъ (Lunt A5)
    • Write оу initially (not ю; Lunt A4) and ꙋ internally; never у
    • Write ꙗ initially (not а); spell the 1sg personal pronoun ꙗзъ
  • Use ESl spellings for *tьrt, etc. (Lunt A6)
  • Use metathesized (SSl) forms for *tort* (never pleophony), except write artificial tret for *tert (e.g., предъ) (Lunt A7–8)
  • Restore where scribe has degeminated /yi, ii/, so выиди even where manuscript says выди

Morphology

  • Write original, rather than analogical, endings. For example, where o-stem and ā-stem nominal endings differ from jo-stem and jā-stem endings, respectively, in more than just hard vs soft spellings, use the original forms. Thus Dsg дѣвици, despite modern Russian девице.
  • Write mnIsg as ESl -ъмь, -ьмь (Lunt B10)
  • Write ESl -ть (front jer) in third-person present-tense verb forms. (Lunt B11)
  • Write the optional -ть (front jer) ending in parentheses on third-person imperfects regardless of whether it is present in the manuscript or not. (Lunt B11, B15)
  • Write -тъ (back jer) in parentheses after 2sg and 2sg aorists for Class I verbs with nasal (e.g., начѧти), /j/ (e.g., бити), and /r/ (e.g., умрети), and the verb жити regardless of whether it is present in the manuscript or not. Write бысть, дасть, есть (front jer). (Schmalstieg p. 95, cf. Lunt OCS grammar, §10.52)
  • DLsg personal pronouns have SSl forms: мьнѣ, тебѣ, себѣ (Lunt B12)
  • Write SSl -ѧ instead of ESl third jat′ in soft endings of nouns and pronouns (fGsg, fNApl, mApl) (Lunt B13)
  • Write SSl -ы, not ESl -а, for mNsg present active participle (Lunt B14)
  • Spell the imperfective suffix as ꙗ (or а, where required as above), that is, contracted несꙗше
  • Spell the imperfect of Class IVA verbs (e.g., носити) with the SSl mutation (ношаше(ть)), instead of the ESl absence of mutation (Lunt B15)
  • Spell long-form mnGsg adjective as -аго but dative as -ому (Lunt B16)
  • The reflexive enclitic сѧ is always written as сѧ (even when the manuscript reads с), and is always written separately from the word that precedes it
  • Dual and plural are used as in the manuscript, even where they are logically incorrect or inconsistent
