Digenis reader: principles for retroversion

Orthography, phonology, and morphology are normalized in conformity with common (not universal) practice ca. 1200–50 (roughly from the marriage of Roman Mstyslavyč The Great to Anna-Euphrosyne (daughter of Byzantine Emperor Isaac II Angelos) ca. 1197 through the end of the reign of their son Danylo in 1264).

See Horace G. Lunt, The language of Rus′ in the eleventh century: some observations about facts and theories, Harvard Ukrainian studies, 12/13 (1988), pp. 276–313 (Appendix at pp. 302–04).

Phonology and orthography

  1. All jers (strong and weak) are written according to etymology, and etymological jers missing from the manuscript are restored. Exception: jers in tense position are always spelled и and ы (not ь or ъ), e.g., новыи, велии, чьстию.
  2. Do not write a jer after prefixes без-, из-, раз-, въз-, об-, and spell voicing assimilations where they occur (cf. Lunt OCS grammar §3.311 ff.). Similarly, spell the prepositions без and из without a jer, and spell voicing assimilations where they occur. Exceptions:
    • Write the prefix объ before и where the manuscript has обо, e.g., объити for manuscript обойти, cf. Lunt OCS grammar §3.312.
    • Write the prefix изъ before и, e.g., изъидошѧ for manuscript изыдоша).
  3. ѣ and е are written according to etymology, except that third jat′ is not used (see below, under Morphology).
  4. Consonant cluster adjustments in the manuscript resulting from the loss of jers in weak position are written according to etymology. E.g., reconstruct събысть сѧ for збы[с]т[ь]ся in the manuscript (voicing assimilation after jer loss) and reconstruct мъногоцѣньныѧ for многоценыя in the manuscript (degemination after jer loss).
  5. The second velar palatalization is written where expected, regardless of whether it is reflected at those locations in the manuscript. The second palatalization of г is spelled з (not ѕ; see Borkovskij and Kuznecov 1963, §21, p. 53).
  6. Do not write й; write и instead.
  7. Undo all apocope, e.g., infinitive and short reflexive pronoun. Restore where scribe has simplified /yi, ii/, so выиди even where manuscript writes выди and добрыи even where the manuscript writes mN/Asg long-form добры.
  8. Write щ from *tj (also *kti) and ж from *dj. (Lunt A3)
    • Lexical exception: охочии
  9. Write Galician/Volynian жч for *zdj/zgj (Lunt A3c)
  10. Plain and soft(ening) vowel letters:
    • Write ꙗ (or а where required as a replacement; see below) and front nasals etymologically, including instead of third jat′ (see below, under Morphology). Do not use modern letterform я

    • Except in situations involving hiatus (e.g., наꙋчити), write only ꙗ, ѩ, ѥ, и, ю (not а, ѧ, е, ы, ꙋ) in the following positions:

      • After vowel letters
      • After palatal /n l r/
    • Write only а, ѧ, е, и, ꙋ (not ꙗ, ѩ, ѥ, ы, ю) after ш, ж, ч, щ, ц
    • Write ѥ, and not о, initially in words like ѥзеро, ѥдинъ (Lunt A5)
    • Write оу initially (not ю; Lunt A4) and ꙋ internally; never у
    • Write ꙗ initially (not а); spell the 1sg personal pronoun ꙗзъ
    • Write ы, and not и after к, г, and х
  11. Use ESl spellings for *tьrt, etc. (Lunt A6)
  12. Use metathesized (SSl) forms for *tort (never pleophony), except write artificial tret for *tert (e.g., предъ). (Lunt A7–8).
    • Lexical exceptions: паволока, оборона
  13. Use SSl spellings for initial *ort- (rat-) (Lunt A8a)
  14. Use ESl spellings for initial *olt- (lot-) (Lunt A8b)
  15. Remove akan′e, replacing it with etymological forms.
  16. Replace Arabic numerals with Cyrillic in the retroversion (but not the manuscript)

Morphology

  1. Write original, rather than analogical, endings. For example, where o-stem and ā-stem nominal endings differ from jo-stem and jā-stem endings, respectively, in more than just hard vs soft spellings, use the original forms. Thus Dsg дѣвици, despite modern Russian девице.
  2. Write o-stem and jo-stem mnIsg as ESl -ъмь, -ьмь, respectively. (Lunt B10)
  3. Write ESl -ть (front jer) in third-person present-tense verb forms. (Lunt B11)
  4. Write the optional -ть (front jer) ending in parentheses on third-person sg and pl (not du) imperfects regardless of whether it is present in the manuscript or not. (Lunt B11, B15)
  5. Write the optional -тъ (back jer) in parentheses after 2sg and 3sg aorists for Class I verbs with nasal (e.g., начѧти), /j/ (e.g., бити), and /r/ (e.g., умрети), and the verb жити regardless of whether it is present in the manuscript or not (cf. Lunt OCS grammar, §10.52).
  6. Write non-past ѥсть and aorist бысть (front jer).
  7. As a (somewhat arbitrary) normalization, write non-past дасть, ѣсть (front jer), but aorist дастъ and ѣстъ (back jer) (cf. Schmalstieg p. 95).
  8. DLsg personal pronouns have SSl forms: мьнѣ, тебѣ, себѣ. (Lunt B12)
  9. Write SSl -ѧ instead of ESl third jat′ in soft endings of nouns and pronouns (fGsg, fNApl, mApl). (Lunt B13)
  10. Write SSl -ы, not ESl -а, for mNsg present active participle of Class I verbs. (Lunt B14)
  11. Spell the imperfective suffix as ꙗ (or а, where required as above), that is, contracted несꙗше.
  12. Spell the imperfect of Class IVA verbs (e.g., носити) with the SSl mutation (ношаше(ть)), instead of the ESl absence of mutation. (Lunt B15)
  13. Spell long-form adjectives as follows:
    • mnGsg always as contracted adjectival ending -аго (Lunt B16)
    • fGsg always as -ыѩ (cf. Lunt B13)
    • all others either contracted adjectival or pronominal ending as in the manuscript (e.g., contracted adjectival Lsg –ѣмь or pronominal –омь)
  14. The reflexive enclitic сѧ is always written as сѧ (even when the manuscript reads с), and is always written separately from the word that precedes it.
  15. Dual:
    • Preserve for forms where duals appear in the manuscripts.
    • Restore for where dual is attested elsewhere in the manuscripts.
    • Restore for natural (or cultural) pairs and two like objects (Vlasto) where dual is attested, or where plural is not attested in dual context, in SDRJaXI-XIV.
  16. We write agreeing participles consistently in our normalization as dictated by meaning, although the manuscripts show the emergence of non-agreeing gerunds in an advanced stage.

Lexicon

  1. Write цьсарь in general (including references to the Amir), but Цѣсарь for the Byzantine emperor, and never modern царь

Syntax

  1. With respect to the copula in nominal sentences, we follow the manuscripts for all persons if the subject is expressed (Vlasto 4.1(a,c) [p. 188–89]). If the subject is not expressed, we include the copula in the first and second person.
  2. For the pf we always include the auxiliary in the 1st and 2nd person; for the 3rd person we follow the manuscripts (Vlasto 3.105 [pp. 159–60], Vlasto 4.1(b) [pp. 188–89]).
  3. If a 1st or 2nd person copula or auxiliary is restored, we remove a pronoun except where emphatic (e.g., we change аще бы ꙗ to аще быхъ in Reading 5).
  4. We retain collective (singular) noun with plural verb where it occurs in the manuscript (e.g., retroversion, following the manuscript: и братиꙗ моꙗ сильни сꙋть in Reading 5).

Abbreviation principles

  1. Spell gender, case, and number as mNsg, etc.
  2. aor (aorist)
  3. pf (perfect)
  4. pf neg (negative perfect, e.g., něstь sъbylъ sę)
  5. ipf (imperfect)
  6. imv (imperative)
  7. pr, pt, fut (present, past, future, but write out "imperfective aor" where needed)
  8. inf (infinitive)
  9. cond (conditional)
  10. act and pass (active, passive)
  11. ppl (participle)
  12. sh and lg (short-form and long-form adj or ppl)
  13. adj, adv, noun, verb, conj (conjunction), int (interjection), prep, pron
  14. pron rel (relative pronoun)
  15. pron pers (personal pronoun)
  16. pron interrog (interrogative pronoun)
  17. dat+inf (dative plus infinitive)
  18. dat abs (dative absolute)
  19. adj poss (possessive adjective)
  20. comp and sup (comparative, superlative)
  21. Gk (Greek)

We include no information about aspect in annotations except imperfective aor where appropriate for byti, or where the aspect might be unexpected (e.g., retroversion, following the manuscript, Reading 5: И слышавъши того гласа.

Other annotation conventions

Adjectives are cited in long form except where those do not exist.
