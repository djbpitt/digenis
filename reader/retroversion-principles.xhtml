Digenis reader: principles for retroversion

Orthography is normalized in conformity with common (not universal) practice ca. 1200–50 (roughly from the marriage of Roman Mstyslavyč The Great to Anna-Euphrosyne (daughter of Byzantine Emperor Isaac II Angelos) ca. 1197 through the end of the reign of their son Danylo in 1264).

See Horace G. Lunt, The language of Rus′ in the eleventh century: some observations about facts and theories, Harvard Ukrainian studies, 12/13 (1988), pp. 276–313 (Appendix at pp. 302–04).

Abbreviation principles

  • Spell gender, case, and number as mNsg, etc.
  • aor (not aorist)
  • pf (not perfect)
  • pf neg (not negative perfect, e.g., něstь sъbylъ sę)
  • ipf (not imperfect)
  • imv (not imperative)
  • pr, pt, fut (not present, past, future, but write out "imperfective aor" where needed)
  • inf (not infinitive)
  • cond (not conditional)
  • act and pass (not active and passive, and not pas [avoid confusion with past])
  • ppl (not participle or other abbreviation)
  • sh and lg for short and long adjectives and participles (not short-form and long-form)
  • adj, adv, noun, verb, conj, int, prep, pron
  • pron rel (not relative pronoun)
  • pron pers (not personal pronoun)
  • pron interrog (not interrogative pronoun)
  • dat+inf
  • dat abs (not dative absolute)
  • adj poss (not possessive adjective)
  • comp and sup (not comparative and superlative)
  • Gk (not Greek)

We include no information about aspect in annotations except imperfective aor where appropriate for byti.

Phonology and orthography

  • All jers (strong and weak) are written according to etymology, and etymological jers missing from the manuscript are restored. Exception: jers in tense position are always spelled и and ы (not ь or ъ), e.g., новыи, велии, чьстию.
  • Do not write a jer after prefixes без-, из-, раз-, въз-, об-, and spell voicing assimilations where they occur (cf. Lunt OCS grammar §3.311 ff.). Similarly, spell the prepositions без and из without a jer, and spell voicing assimilations where they occur. Exceptions:
    • Write the prefix объ before и where the manuscript has обо, e.g., объити for manuscript обойти, cf. Lunt OCS grammar §3.312.
    • Write the prefix изъ before и, e.g., изъидошѧ for manuscript изыдоша).
  • ѣ and е are written according to etymology, except that third jat′ is not used (see below, under Morphology).
  • Consonant cluster adjustments in the manuscript resulting from the loss of jers in weak position are written according to etymology. E.g., reconstruct събысть сѧ for збы[с]т[ь]ся in the manuscript (voicing assimilation after jer loss) and reconstruct мъногоцѣньныѧ for многоценыя in the manuscript (degemination after jer loss).
  • The second velar palatalization is written where expected, regardless of whether it is reflected at those locations in the manuscript. The second palatalization of г is spelled з (not ѕ; see Borkovskij and Kuznecov 1963, §21, p. 53).
  • Do not write й; write и instead.
  • Undo all apocope, e.g., infinitive and short reflexive pronoun. Restore where scribe has simplified /yi, ii/, so выиди even where manuscript writes выди and добрыи even where the manuscript writes mN/Asg long-form добры.
  • Write щ from *tj (also *kti) and ж from *dj. (Lunt A3)
    • Lexical exception: охочии
  • Write Galician/Volynian жч for *zdj/zgj (Lunt 3c)
  • Plain and soft(ening) vowel letters:
    • Write ꙗ (or а where required as a replacement; see below) and front nasals etymologically, including instead of third jat′ (see below, under Morphology). Do not use modern letterform я

    • Except in situations involving hiatus (e.g., наꙋчити), write only ꙗ, ѩ, ѥ, и, ю (not а, ѧ, е, ы, ꙋ) in the following positions:

      • After vowel letters
      • After palatal /n l r/
    • Write only а, ѧ, е, и, ꙋ (not ꙗ, ѩ, ѥ, ы, ю) after ш, ж, ч, щ, ц
    • Write ѥ, and not о, initially in words like ѥзеро, ѥдинъ (Lunt A5)
    • Write оу initially (not ю; Lunt A4) and ꙋ internally; never у
    • Write ꙗ initially (not а); spell the 1sg personal pronoun ꙗзъ
    • Write ы, and not и after к, г, and х
  • Use ESl spellings for *tьrt, etc. (Lunt A6)
  • Use metathesized (SSl) forms for *tort (never pleophony), except write artificial tret for *tert (e.g., предъ). (Lunt A7–8).
    • Lexical exceptions: паволока, оборона)
  • Use SSl spellings for initial *ort- (rat-) (Lunt 8a)
  • Use ESl spellings for initial *olt- (lot-) (Lunt 8b)
  • Remove akan′e, replacing it with etymological forms.
  • Replace Arabic numerals with Cyrillic in the retroversion (but not the manuscript)

Morphology

  • Write original, rather than analogical, endings. For example, where o-stem and ā-stem nominal endings differ from jo-stem and jā-stem endings, respectively, in more than just hard vs soft spellings, use the original forms. Thus Dsg дѣвици, despite modern Russian девице.
  • Write o-stem and jo-stem mnIsg as ESl -ъмь, -ьмь, respectively. (Lunt B10)
  • Write ESl -ть (front jer) in third-person present-tense verb forms. (Lunt B11)
  • Write the optional -ть (front jer) ending in parentheses on third-person sg and pl (not du) imperfects regardless of whether it is present in the manuscript or not. (Lunt B11, B15)
  • Write the optional -тъ (back jer) in parentheses after 2sg and 3sg aorists for Class I verbs with nasal (e.g., начѧти), /j/ (e.g., бити), and /r/ (e.g., умрети), and the verb жити regardless of whether it is present in the manuscript or not (cf. Lunt OCS grammar, §10.52).
  • Write non-past ѥсть and aorist бысть (front jer).
  • As a (somewhat arbitrary) normalization, write non-past дасть, ѣсть (front jer), but aorist дастъ and ѣстъ (back jer) (cf. Schmalstieg p. 95).
  • DLsg personal pronouns have SSl forms: мьнѣ, тебѣ, себѣ. (Lunt B12)
  • Write SSl -ѧ instead of ESl third jat′ in soft endings of nouns and pronouns (fGsg, fNApl, mApl). (Lunt B13)
  • Write SSl -ы, not ESl -а, for mNsg present active participle of Class I verbs. (Lunt B14)
  • Spell the imperfective suffix as ꙗ (or а, where required as above), that is, contracted несꙗше.
  • Spell the imperfect of Class IVA verbs (e.g., носити) with the SSl mutation (ношаше(ть)), instead of the ESl absence of mutation. (Lunt B15)
  • Spell long-form adjectives as follows:
    • mnGsg always as contracted adjectival ending -аго (Lunt B16)
    • all others either contracted adjectival or pronominal ending as in the manuscript (e.g., contracted adjectival Lsg –ѣмь or pronominal –омь)
  • The reflexive enclitic сѧ is always written as сѧ (even when the manuscript reads с), and is always written separately from the word that precedes it.
  • We write duals consistently in our normalization as dictated by the meaning, although duals are not used at all consistently in the manuscripts.
  • We write agreeing participles consistently in our normalization as dictated by meaning, although the manuscripts show the emergence of non-agreeing gerunds in an advanced stage.

Lexicon

  • Write цьсарь in general (including references to the Amir), but Цѣсарь for the Byzantine emperor, and never modern царь
