Digenis reader: principles for retroversion

Phonology and orthography

  • All jers (strong and weak) are written according to etymology, and etymological jers missing from the manuscript are restored
  • Infinitive-final -и is spelled even where the manuscript has ь
  • Consonant cluster adjustments resulting from the loss of jers in weak position, such as voicing assimilation (e.g., збысться) or degemination (e.g., многоценыя), are written according to etymology
  • ѥ is not used; write only е
  • оу or ꙋ are not used; write only у or ю
  • Write only softening vowel letters я, и, ю after palatals, including ц
  • ѧ and ꙗ are not used; write only а or я (Lunt A2)
  • Write initial *ju as у (Lunt A4)
  • Use ESl spellings for *tьrt, etc. (Lunt A6)
  • Write artificial tret for *tert (e.g., предъ) (Lunt A7a)

Morphology

  • Write mnIsg as ESl -ъмь, -ьмь (Lunt B10)
  • Write ESl front jer in third-person present-tense verb forms (Lunt B11)
  • DLsg personal pronouns have SSl forms: мьнѣ, тебѣ, себѣ (Lunt B12)
  • Write SSl -ѧ instead of ESl third jat′ in soft endings of nouns and pronouns (fGsg, fNApl, mApl) (Lunt B13)
  • Dual and plural are used as in the manuscript, even where they are logically incorrect or inconsistent
