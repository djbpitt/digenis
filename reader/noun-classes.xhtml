Declension classes

Some early Slavic nouns can be assigned reliably to specific minor declension classes (that is, classes other than o/jo- and ā/jā-stem), but others are less certain because the manuscript and etymological evidence is skimpy or inconsistent. The following lists of nouns by declension class are drawn primarily from the secondary sources listed at the bottom of this page. Most of these nouns also exhibit forms of other declension classes; some are reliably attributable to minor classes, while others show only sporadic minor-class forms. Forms below have been normalized according to the same principles as our textual normalization.

ŭ-stem (all masculine)

боръ coniferous wood, волъ ox, вьрхъ top, гласъ voice, гръмъ bush, даръ gift, домъ house, дꙋбъ oak, дългъ debt, ледъ ice, медъ honey, миръ world, олъ ale, пиръ feast, полъ half, разъ stroke, родъ race, рѧдъ row, садъ plantation; garden, санъ (high) rank, станъ camp, сынъ son, сынъ tower, чинъ rank, ꙗдъ poison

ĭ-stem masculine

боль sick man, вепрь wild boar, гвоздь nail, гладь hunger, голꙋбь dove, господь lord, гость guest, гусь goose, гъртань throat, деверь husband’s brother, желудь acorn, звѣрь beast, зѧть bridegroom, къметь knight; retainer, лебедь swan, лось elk, moose, лысть calf (of leg), людиѥ people (pl only), маломощь cripple; beggar (m, but Isg -ью), медвѣдь bear, моль moth, огнь fire, пꙋть road, рысь lynx, тать thief, тьсть wife’s father, оушидь fugitive, чьрвь worm, оугль coal

ĭ-stem feminine

Nouns with the suffix -ость; the numerals трье (inflected for gender) three пѧть five, шесть six, седмь seven, осмь eight, девѧть nine, кость bone, нощь night

ū-stem (all feminine)

брады ax, букы writing; letter (of the alphabet), желы tortoise, жьрны millstone, зълы husband’s sister, кры blood, локы pond, любы love, мъркы carrot, неплоды barren woman, редькы radish, свекры husband’s mother, смокы fig, тыкы pumpkin, ꙋты duck, хоругы flag, цьркы church, цѣлы cure, ѧтры wife’s sister

Consonant-stem nouns (all genders)

Indo-European had several categories of consonant-stem nouns, most of which were assimilated to other classes in Slavic, although some of them left traces of their original forms as irregularities (e.g., Rus небо has plural небеса). The t-stems, in particular, are almost completely assimilated into the ĭ-stems in the earliest Slavic attestations. The ū-stems are regarded as consonant stems because Indo-European long *ū alternated with *ŭv (e.g., the original Nsg of Rus любовь was любы).

n-stem masculine

Those attested with NAsg -ы (even if only in OCS) are listed accordingly.

гребень comb, дьнь day, ремень strap, ѥлень stag, deer, камы stone, корень root, кремень flint, мишень target, перстень ring, пламы flame, плетень hurdle, ревень rhubarb, ремы stag, сажень fathom, степень degree, стремень stirrup, шершень hornet, ясень ash, ꙗчмень barley

n-stem neuter

бремѧ burden, времѧ time, вымѧ udder, знамѧ banner, имѧ name, писмѧ letter, племѧ tribe, сѣмѧ seed, тѣмѧ crown (of head), чисмѧ numeral

s-stem (all neuter)

диво miracle, древо tree, дѣло work, коло wheel, истесѣ, истеса kidneys; loins (du and pl only), лице (oblique личес-) face, люто misdeed, небо sky; heaven, око (oblique очес-, irreg du очи) eye, слово word, тѣло body, оухо (oblique оушес-, irreg du уши) ear, чꙋдо wonder

r-stem (all feminine)

дъщи daughter, мати mother

t-stem (all masculine)

дегъть pitch, локъть elbow, когъть claw, копъть soot, ногъть fingernail, пазнегъть claw, печать seal

nt-stem (all neuter)

Productive -ѧт- suffix forms names of young living creatures.

жребѧ foal, клюшѧ draft animal, козьлѧ kid, овьчѧ lamb, осьлѧ young ass, отрочѧ child, телѧ calf
